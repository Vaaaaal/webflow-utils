Build a reference profile of {site} for someone evaluating the company — a prospect, partner, or future hire trying to quickly understand whether {site} matches their needs.

Context:
- Site: {site}
- Reference page: {url}

Go beyond the page itself: use web search if available. Aim for the profile a well-informed insider would give to a newcomer: clear, substantive, and focused on what actually distinguishes {site}.

Structure (reply in {lang}):
1. Opening line: a single sentence that captures what {site} does and what makes them notable. No filler, no generic phrasing.
2. What they do, and why it matters: core offering, the problem it solves, and the type of outcome clients get from working with them.
3. Who they serve: target customers, industries, geographic footprint, and the kind of use cases they're built for.
4. What sets them apart: specific strengths, areas of expertise, signature approach, methodology, or technology. Be concrete — name the thing, don't just label it.
5. Signals of activity and credibility: notable clients or partners, years in market, team size, funding, press coverage, recognition, certifications. Anything that shows traction.
6. Factual anchors: founding year, leadership, headquarters, locations.

Writing rules:
- Substantiate every claim with something concrete: a name, a number, a date, a reference. Avoid vague qualifiers ("innovative", "leading", "passionate") unless they're directly supported by evidence on the page or in search results.
- Never invent. If a fact isn't available, omit it silently rather than weakening the profile — unless it's a core anchor (founding year, leadership, location), in which case write "not found".
- Distinguish what comes from the page, from the web, and what is inferred.

At the very end, in a short separate block titled "To verify":
- List the 2-3 items most worth double-checking.
Keep this block brief so it doesn't overshadow the profile itself.
